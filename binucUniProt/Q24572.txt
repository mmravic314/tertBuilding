CC   -!- FUNCTION: Core histone-binding subunit that may target chromatin
CC       assembly factors, chromatin remodeling factors and histone
CC       deacetylases to their histone substrates in a manner that is
CC       regulated by nucleosomal DNA. Component of several complexes which
CC       regulate chromatin metabolism. These include the chromatin
CC       assembly factor 1 (CAF-1) complex, which is required for chromatin
CC       assembly following DNA replication and DNA repair; the nucleosome
CC       remodeling and deacetylase complex (the NuRD complex), which
CC       promotes transcriptional repression by histone deacetylation and
CC       nucleosome remodeling; the nucleosome remodeling factor (NURF)
CC       complex, which catalyzes ATP-dependent nucleosome sliding and
CC       facilitates transcription of chromatin; and the polycomb group
CC       (PcG) repressor complex ESC-E(Z), which promotes repression of
CC       homeotic genes during development. Also required for
CC       transcriptional repression of E2F target genes by E2f2 and Rbf or
CC       Rbf2. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12490953, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15456884,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8887645, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9419341,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9784495}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Probably binds directly to helix 1 of the histone fold of
CC       histone H4, a region that is not accessible when H4 is in
CC       chromatin. Self associates. Associates with chromatin. Component
CC       of the CAF-1 complex, composed of Caf1, Caf1-105 and Caf1-180.
CC       Within the CAF-1 complex, Caf1-180 interacts directly with both
CC       Caf1 and Caf1-105. Component of the NuRD complex, composed of at
CC       least Caf1, Mi-2, MTA1-like and Rpd3. Within the NuRD complex,
CC       Caf1 may interact directly with Mi-2, MTA1-like and Rpd3. The NuRD
CC       complex may also associate with the methyl-DNA binding protein
CC       MBD-like via Caf1 and Mi-2. Component of the NURF complex,
CC       composed of Caf1, E(bx), Nurf-38 and Iswi. Component of the
CC       Esc/E(z) complex, composed of Caf1, esc, E(z), Su(z)1, and
CC       possibly Pho1. The Esc/E(z) complex may also associate with Pcl
CC       and Rpd3 during early embryogenesis. Interacts with Rbf and Rbf2.
CC       Component of the DREAM complex at least composed of Myb, Caf1,
CC       mip40, mip120, mip130, E2f2, Dp, Rbf, Rbf2, lin-52, Rpd3 and
CC       l(3)mbt. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11124122,
