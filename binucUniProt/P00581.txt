CC   -!- FUNCTION: Replicates viral genomic DNA. This polymerase possesses
CC       two enzymatic activities: DNA synthesis (polymerase) and an
CC       exonucleolytic activity that degrades single-stranded DNA in the
CC       3'- to 5'-direction. Non-processive DNA polymerase that achieves
CC       processivity by binding to host thioredoxin (TrxA). This
CC       interaction increases the rate of dNTP incorporation to yield a
CC       processivity of approximately 800 nucleotides (nt) per binding
CC       event. Interacts with DNA helicase gp4 to coordinate nucleotide
CC       polymerization with unwinding of the DNA. The leading strand is
CC       synthesized continuously while synthesis of the lagging strand
CC       requires the synthesis of oligoribonucleotides by the primase
CC       domain of gp4. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15292168,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21606333, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2703498,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9218486}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Deoxynucleoside triphosphate + DNA(n) =
CC       diphosphate + DNA(n+1). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24591606}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Composed of two subunits. One is encoded by the phage and
CC       the other is encoded by the host thioredoxin. Interacts with
CC       helicase/primase gp4; this interaction is essential for the
CC       coordination of DNA unwinding and nucleotide polymerization on
CC       duplex DNA. Interacts with the ssDNA-binding protein gp2.5.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15795374, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9218486}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the DNA polymerase type-A family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
