CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the synthesis of the hydroxymethylpyrimidine
CC       phosphate (HMP-P) moiety of thiamine from aminoimidazole ribotide
CC       (AIR) in a radical S-adenosyl-L-methionine (SAM)-dependent
CC       reaction. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18332905}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 5-amino-1-(5-phospho-D-ribosyl)imidazole + S-
CC       adenosyl-L-methionine = 4-amino-2-methyl-5-phosphomethylpyrimidine
CC       + 5'-deoxyadenosine + L-methionine + formate + CO.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=[4Fe-4S] cluster; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:49883;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325,
CC         ECO:0000305|PubMed:24161603};
CC       Note=Binds 1 [4Fe-4S] cluster per subunit. The cluster is
CC       coordinated with 3 cysteines and an exchangeable S-adenosyl-L-
CC       methionine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor biosynthesis; thiamine diphosphate biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24161603}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Plastid, chloroplast stroma
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16207701, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18332905, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18431481,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20061580}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Strongly expressed in cotyledons, leaves,
CC       flowers and siliques, and, to a lower extent, in roots
CC       (PubMed:18048325, PubMed:25014715). Expressed in all green
CC       tissues, but not in roots, seeds, and petals (PubMed:23341335).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23341335,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25014715}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: First detected in five-days seedlings. In
CC       seedlings, mostly present at the root tips and in the jointed
CC       section between the hypocotyl and root. In two-leaves seedlings,
CC       expressed in leaves, cotyledons and vascular bundles of
CC       hypocotyls, and very weakly, in the roots. In flowers, detected in
CC       sepals, filaments and pistil tips, but not in petals. Later
CC       accumulates in the jointed region between the silique and silique
CC       stem, and in the tips of siliques. Decreasing expression during
CC       seed development (PubMed:25014715). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18332905, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25014715}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Circadian-regulation (PubMed:23341335). Down-regulated
CC       by extrinsic thiamine, via a vitamin B1 derivative thiamine
CC       pyrophosphate (TPP)-sensing riboswitch regulation. Detected in
CC       both dark and light grown seedlings; increased progressively after
CC       transfer of etiolated seedlings to light. Up-regulated by salt,
CC       osmotic stress and abscisic acid (PubMed:22214485).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16675665, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17993623,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18332905,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22214485, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23341335}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Albino (white leaves) and lethal under
CC       normal culture conditions, probably due to an impairment in
CC       thiamine biosynthesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18048325,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18332905}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ThiC family. {ECO:0000305}.
