CC   -!- FUNCTION: Proton-translocating inorganic pyrophosphatase that
CC       contributes to the transtonoplast (from cytosol to vacuole lumen)
CC       H(+)-electrochemical potential difference. It establishes a proton
CC       gradient of similar and often greater magnitude than the H(+)-
CC       ATPase on the same membrane. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10477275,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22456709, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2555340,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9489011}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Diphosphate + H(2)O = 2 phosphate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10477275, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22456709}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by excess pyrophosphate as well as
CC       excess Mg(2+). Inhibition by ATP, GTP, and CTP is reversed by
CC       increasing the Mg(2+) concentration. This suggests that the
CC       substrate is a particular metal complex such as MgPPi(2-).
CC       Modification of Asp-283 with DCCD abolishes pyrophosphatase
CC       activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10477275,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2555340}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22456709}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Vacuole membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10477275, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22456709,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2555340, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9489011}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10477275,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22456709, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2555340,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9489011}. Note=Tonoplast membrane.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Detected in the hypocotyl, with the highest
CC       expression in the rapidly expanding segment of the hypocotyl and
CC       lower expression in older, less rapidly expanding parts of the
CC       hypocotyl. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9489011}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Has 16 transmembrane helices and a large cytoplasmic
CC       domain that contains the active site.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22456709}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Has few direct interactions with pyrophosphate.
CC       Interacts with the substrate via divalent metal cations, such as
CC       magnesium ions, that are bound to the pyrophosphate.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the H(+)-translocating pyrophosphatase
CC       (TC 3.A.10) family. K(+)-stimulated subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
