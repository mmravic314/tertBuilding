CC   -!- FUNCTION: Copper-containing oxidase that catalyzes both the o-
CC       hydroxylation of monophenols and the subsequent oxidation of the
CC       resulting o-diphenols into reactive o-quinones, which evolve
CC       spontaneously to produce intermediates, which associate in dark
CC       brown pigments. Involved in the initial step of melanin synthesis.
CC       Melanins constitute a mechanism of defense and resistance to
CC       stress such as UV radiations, free radicals, gamma rays,
CC       dehydratation and extreme temperatures, and contribute to the
CC       fungal cell-wall resistance against hydrolytic enzymes in avoiding
CC       cellular lysis. Fungal pigments are also involved in the formation
CC       and stability of spores (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
