CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates the transient calcium-dependent interaction of
CC       PYR/PYL/RCAR abscisic acid (ABA) receptors with the plasma
CC       membrane and thus regulates ABA sensitivity (PubMed:25465408).
CC       Stimulates the GTPase/ATPase activities of YchF1, and regulates
CC       its subcellular localization. Promotes tolerance towards salinity
CC       stress by limiting the accumulation of reactive oxygen species
CC       (ROS) (PubMed:23550829). Promotes resistance to bacterial
CC       pathogens (e.g. Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae and P. syringae pv.
CC       tomato DC3000) (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6YWF1,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25465408}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Binds to PYR/PYL/RCAR abscisic acid intracellular
CC       receptors in an ABA-independent manner, both at the plasma
CC       membrane and in the nucleus. Interacts directly with PYR1, PYL1,
CC       PYL4, PYL6 and PYL8. Binds phospholipids in a Ca(2+)-dependent
CC       manner (PubMed:25465408). Interacts with YchF1 (PubMed:23550829).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23550829, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25465408}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25465408}.
CC       Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25465408}. Cytoplasm, cytosol
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23550829}. Note=Localized mainly in the
CC       cytosol under NaCl treatment, but translocates to the plasma
CC       membrane upon wounding. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23550829}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: When associated with disruption in CAR1,
CC       CAR5 and CAR9 genes, reduced sensitivity to abscisic acid (ABA)
CC       during seedling establishment and root growth regulation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25465408}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the plant CAR protein family.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:25465408}.
