CC   -!- FUNCTION: Encapsidates the genome, protecting it from nucleases.
CC       The encapsidated genomic RNA is termed the nucleocapsid (NC).
CC       Serves as template for viral transcription and replication. The
CC       increased presence of protein N in host cell does not seem to
CC       trigger the switch from transcription to replication as observed
CC       in other negative strain RNA viruses. Disables the host innate
CC       defense by interfering with beta interferon (IFNB) production
CC       through the inhibition of host IRF3 phosphorylation and activation
CC       by host IKBKE. Through the interaction with host IKBKE, strongly
CC       inhibits the phosphorylation and nuclear translocation of IRF3, a
CC       protein involved in IFN activation pathway, leading to the
CC       inhibition of IFNB and IRF3-dependent promoters activation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homomultimerizes to form the nucleocapsid. Binds to viral
CC       genomic RNA. Interacts with glycoprotein G2. Interacts with
CC       protein Z; this interaction probably directs the encapsidated
CC       genome to budding sites. Interacts with protein L; this
CC       interaction does not interfere with Z-L interaction. Interacts
CC       with host IKBKE (via Protein kinase domain); the interaction
CC       inhibits IKBKE kinase activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15019244,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22532683}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Virion. Host cytoplasm.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the arenaviridae nucleocapsid protein
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
