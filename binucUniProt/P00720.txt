CC   -!- FUNCTION: Endolysin with lysozyme activity that degrades host
CC       peptidoglycans and participates with the holin and spanin proteins
CC       in the sequential events which lead to the programmed host cell
CC       lysis releasing the mature viral particles. Once the holin has
CC       permeabilized the host cell membrane, the endolysin can reach the
CC       periplasm and break down the peptidoglycan layer.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22389108}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Hydrolysis of (1->4)-beta-linkages between N-
CC       acetylmuramic acid and N-acetyl-D-glucosamine residues in a
CC       peptidoglycan and between N-acetyl-D-glucosamine residues in
CC       chitodextrins.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Host cytoplasm {ECO:0000305}. Note=The
CC       endolysin is cytoplasmic, but can reach the periplasmic space with
CC       the help of the holins which disrupt the host cell membrane.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the glycosyl hydrolase 24 family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
