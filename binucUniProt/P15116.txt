CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cadherins are calcium-dependent cell adhesion proteins.
CC       They preferentially interact with themselves in a homophilic
CC       manner in connecting cells; cadherins may thus contribute to the
CC       sorting of heterogeneous cell types. Acts as a regulator of neural
CC       stem cells quiescence by mediating anchorage of neural stem cells
CC       to ependymocytes in the adult subependymal zone: upon cleavage by
CC       MMP24, CDH2-mediated anchorage is affected, leading to modulate
CC       neural stem cell quiescence (PubMed:24952463). CDH2 may be
CC       involved in neuronal recognition mechanism. In hippocampal
CC       neurons, may regulate dendritic spine density.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11433297, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17988630,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24952463}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with CDCP1 (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       PCDH8; this complex may also include TAOK2 (By similarity). The
CC       interaction with PCDH8 may lead to internalization through
CC       TAOK2/p38 MAPK pathway. Identified in a complex containing FGFR4,
CC       NCAM1, CDH2, PLCG1, FRS2, SRC, SHC1, GAP43 and CTTN. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11433297}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P18031:PTPN1 (xeno); NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-397974, EBI-968788;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane; Single-pass type I membrane
CC       protein.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed at all stages of testicular
CC       development with highest levels found in testes of 21-day-old
CC       mice. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8879495}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Three calcium ions are usually bound at the interface of
CC       each cadherin domain and rigidify the connections, imparting a
CC       strong curvature to the full-length ectodomain. Calcium-binding
CC       sites are occupied sequentially in the order of site 3, then site
CC       2 and site 1.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleaved by MMP24. Ectodomain cleavage leads to the generation
CC       of a soluble 90 kDa amino-terminal soluble fragment and a 45 kDa
CC       membrane-bound carboxy-terminal fragment 1 (CTF1), which is
CC       further cleaved by gamma-secretase into a 35 kDa. Cleavage in
CC       neural stem cells by MMP24 affects CDH2-mediated anchorage of
CC       neural stem cells to ependymocytes in the adult subependymal zone,
CC       leading to modulate neural stem cell quiescence.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19805319, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24952463}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Embryonic lethality.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24952463}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 5 cadherin domains. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU00043}.
