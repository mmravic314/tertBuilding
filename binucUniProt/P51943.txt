CC   -!- FUNCTION: Essential for the control of the cell cycle at the G1/S
CC       (start) and the G2/M (mitosis) transitions.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10068472}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with the CDK1 and CDK2 protein kinases to form
CC       a serine/threonine kinase holoenzyme complex. The cyclin subunit
CC       imparts substrate specificity to the complex. In the testis,
CC       interacts only with CDK2. When associated with CDK2 (but not with
CC       CDK1), interacts with SCAPER (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P11440:Cdk1; NbExp=2; IntAct=EBI-846980, EBI-846949;
CC       P97377:Cdk2; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-846980, EBI-847048;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus. Cytoplasm. Note=Cytoplasmic when
CC       associated with SCAPER. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Ubiquitous. In the testis, expressed in germ
CC       cells and in the ovary, in both germline and somatic cells.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Accumulates steadily during G2 and is
CC       abruptly destroyed at mitosis. Expressed in spermatogonia and is
CC       most abundant in preleptotene spermatocytes, cells which will
CC       enter the meiotic pathway.
CC   -!- PTM: Polyubiquitinated via 'Lys-11'-linked ubiquitin by the
CC       anaphase-promoting complex (APC/C), leading to its degradation by
CC       the proteasome. Deubiquitinated and stabilized by USP37 enables
CC       entry into S phase (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the cyclin family. Cyclin AB subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
