CC   -!- FUNCTION: Importin beta subunit that functions in nuclear protein
CC       import through association with the importin alpha subunit, which
CC       binds to the classical nuclear localization signal (cNLS) in cargo
CC       substrates (PubMed:7622450). Docking of the importin/substrate
CC       complex to the nuclear pore complex (NPC) is mediated by importin
CC       beta through binding to nucleoporin FxFG repeats and the complex
CC       is subsequently translocated through the pore by an energy
CC       requiring, Ran-dependent mechanism (PubMed:8521485). At the
CC       nucleoplasmic side of the NPC, GTP-Ran binds to importin beta and
CC       the three components separate, leading to release of the cargo
CC       (PubMed:15864302). Importin alpha and beta are re-exported from
CC       the nucleus to the cytoplasm where GTP hydrolysis releases Ran
CC       from importin beta. The directionality of nuclear import is
CC       thought to be conferred by an asymmetric distribution of the GTP-
CC       and GDP-bound forms of Ran between the cytoplasm and nucleus
CC       (PubMed:11423015). Mediates the nuclear import of histones H2A and
CC       H2B (PubMed:11309407). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11309407,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15864302, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7622450,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8521485, ECO:0000305|PubMed:11423015}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a complex with the importin alpha subunit
CC       (SRP1/KAP60) (PubMed:7622450). Interacts with Ran (GSP1);
CC       interacts specifically with the GTP-bound form of Ran (GTP-Ran),
CC       protecting it from GTP hydrolysis and nucleotide exchange
CC       (PubMed:8621381, PubMed:9321403). Interacts with nucleoporin NUP1
CC       (PubMed:8521485, PubMed:15878174). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15878174,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7622450, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8521485,
