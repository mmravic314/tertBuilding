CC   -!- FUNCTION: Inner membrane protein required for the insertion and/or
CC       proper folding and/or complex formation of integral inner membrane
CC       proteins. Involved in integration of membrane proteins that insert
CC       dependently and independently of the Sec translocase complex, as
CC       well as at least 2 lipoproteins. Its own insertion requires SRP
CC       and is Sec translocase-dependent. Essential for the integration of
CC       Sec-dependent subunit a of the F(0)ATP synthase, FtsQ and SecE
CC       proteins and for Sec-independent subunit c of the F(0)ATP
CC       synthase, M13 phage procoat and the N-terminus of leader peptidase
CC       Lep. Probably interacts directly with Sec-independent substrates.
CC       Sec-dependent protein FtsQ interacts first with SecY then
CC       subsequently with YidC. Sec-dependent LacY and MalF require YidC
CC       to acquire tertiary structure and stability, a chaperone-like
CC       function, but not for membrane insertion. Stable maltose transport
CC       copmplex formation (MalFGK(2)) also requires YidC. Partially
CC       complements a Streptococcus mutans yidC2 disruption mutant.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10675323, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10949305,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12724529, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12950181,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15067017, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15140892,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17073462, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18456666}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with SecD and SecF. Component of the SecDFyajC
CC       complex, a heterotetrameric translocase complex. Specifically
CC       interacts with transmembrane segments of nascent integral membrane
CC       proteins during membrane integration. Found in 3 different
CC       complexes in inner membrane preparations (PubMed:16079137). Can be
CC       cross-linked to FtsH, in the larger FtsH/HflKC complex.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10675323, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11415986,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12068816, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18387365}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P03623:VIII (xeno); NbExp=5; IntAct=EBI-6400779, EBI-8482910;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11821429, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11821429,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}. Note=Predominantly localized at cell
CC       poles at all stages of cell growth.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: At mid-exponential phase in strain MC4100 there are
CC       about 2500 copies per cell (at protein level).
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Most of the large periplasmic domain (residues 24-264) is
CC       not required for either Sec-dependent or Sec-independent protein
CC       insertion. However, residues 265-346, the C-terminal part of the
CC       large periplasmic domain, are required for both Sec-dependent and
CC       Sec-independent protein insertion.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Lethality. Upon depletion experiments
CC       insertion of Sec translocase-independent integral membrane
CC       proteins ceases. Translocation of Sec-dependent protein decreases
CC       to a lesser extent. Also leads to decreased targeting and/or
CC       translocation of Lpp and BRP lipoproteins. Both spoIIIJ and yqjG
CC       of B.subtilis functionally complement yidC depletion, whereas
CC       Streptococcus mutans yidC1 and yidC2 only partially complement
CC       depletion. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10949305,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11415986, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15067017,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21778229}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the OXA1/ALB3/YidC family. Type 1
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
