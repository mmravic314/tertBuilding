CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a role in hormone and neurotransmitter exocytosis
CC       (PubMed:17301173, PubMed:18167541, PubMed:20484665,
CC       PubMed:22411134). Potentially involved in docking of synaptic
CC       vesicles at presynaptic active zones. May mediate Ca(2+)-
CC       regulation of exocytosis acrosomal reaction in sperm.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17301173, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18167541,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20484665, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22411134}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via C-terminus) with KCNB1 (via C-terminus);
CC       the interaction increases in a calcium-dependent manner and
CC       induces a pore-independent enhancement of exocytosis in
CC       neuroendocrine cells, chromaffin cells, pancreatic beta cells and
CC       from the soma of dorsal root ganglia (DRG) neurons
CC       (PubMed:17301173, PubMed:18167541, PubMed:20484665,
CC       PubMed:22411134). Interacts with OTOF, SYTL4, VAPA, SYBU and LGI3.
CC       Found in a complex with VAMP8 and SNAP23. Interacts with SYT6 and
CC       SYT8; the interaction is Ca(2+)-dependent (By similarity). Part of
CC       the SNARE core complex containing SNAP25, VAMP2 and STX1A. This
CC       complex binds to CPLX1. Binds STXBP6. Found in a ternary complex
CC       with STX1A and SNAP25. Interacts with SLC6A4. Interacts with
CC       STXBP1 and DAPK1. {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10336434,
