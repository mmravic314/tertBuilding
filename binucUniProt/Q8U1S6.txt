CC   -!- FUNCTION: CRISPR (clustered regularly interspaced short
CC       palindromic repeat), is an adaptive immune system that provides
CC       protection against mobile genetic elements (viruses, transposable
CC       elements and conjugative plasmids). CRISPR clusters contain
CC       sequences complementary to antecedent mobile elements and target
CC       invading nucleic acids. CRISPR clusters are transcribed and
CC       processed into CRISPR RNA (crRNA), formerly called psiRNA
CC       (prokaryotic silencing) in this organism. Part of the Cmr
CC       ribonucleoprotein complex which has divalent cation-dependent
CC       endoribonuclease activity specific for ssRNA complementary to the
CC       crRNA (target RNA), generating 5' hydroxy- and 3' phosphate or 2'-
CC       3' cyclic phosphate termini. Cmr4 is probably the subunit that
CC       cleaves target RNA (PubMed:25280103). Cmr complex does not cleave
CC       ssDNA complementary to the crRNA. Cleavage of target RNA is guided
CC       by the crRNA; substrate cleavage occurs a fixed distance (14 nt)
CC       from the 3' end of the crRNA. In vitro reconstitution shows Cmr1-2
CC       and Cmr5 are not absolutely necessary for target cleavage
CC       (PubMed:19945378). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19945378,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22405013, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25280103}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the type III-B Cmr ribonucleoprotein (RNP)
CC       complex, an elongated RNP with Cmr2 and Cmr3 as the base, with
CC       Cmr4 and Cmr5 forming a helical core along the mature crRNA (39 or
CC       45 nt in length), while the complex is capped by Cmr6 and Cmr1.
CC       The 5' end of the crRNA is bound to Cmr2 and Cmr3, while Cmr6 and
CC       a Cmr1 sbunit (Cmr1-1 or Cmr1-2) cap the 3' end of the crRNA. The
CC       target RNA lies antiparallel to the crRNA, with its 5' end near
CC       Cmr1 and Cmr6 and its 3' end near Cmr2 and Cmr3; major target
CC       cleavage occurs nears the junction of Cmr1/Cmr6 and Cmr4/Cmr, with
CC       minor cleavage occurring at 6 nt intervals which coincide with the
CC       proposed spacing of Cmr4 subunits (PubMed:24119404,
CC       PubMed:25280103). Forms a 1:1 complex with Cmr3 (PubMed:23583914,
CC       PubMed:23395183). The Cmr2-Cmr3 complex non-specifically binds ss-
CC       target RNA and crRNA (PubMed:23583914). Interacts with Cmr3, Cmr4
CC       and Cmr5 (PubMed:25280103). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19945378,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23395183, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23583914,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24119404, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25280103}.
