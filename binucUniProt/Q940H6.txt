CC   -!- FUNCTION: Activator of the abscisic acid (ABA) signaling pathway
CC       that regulates numerous ABA responses, such as stomata closure in
CC       response to drought, plant pathogens, or decreases in atmospheric
CC       relative humidity (RH). Involved in the resistance to drought by
CC       avoiding water loss. Required for the stomata closure mediated by
CC       pathogen-associated molecular pattern (PAMPs) (e.g. flg22 and LPS)
CC       of pathogenic bacteria such as P.syringae pv. tomato (Pst) and
CC       E.coli O157:H7. As a plant defense process, stomata are closed
CC       transiently in order to limit invaders, but actively reopened by
CC       bacteria after a few hours; virulent strains (e.g. Pst DC3000) are
CC       more efficient than avirulent strains (e.g. Pst DC3000 AvrRpt2) in
CC       reopening stomata. Mediates the phosphorylation and activation of
CC       the S-type anion efflux channel SLAC1, and thus promotes stomata
CC       closure. Essential for stomatal closure in response to reactive
CC       oxygen species (ROS). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12047634,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12468729, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12514244,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15064385, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16365038,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16682349, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16766677,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16959575, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17307925,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19955405, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19955427,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20128877}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a protein = ADP + a phosphoprotein.

CC       humidity. Repressed by PP2CA independently of its phosphatase
CC       activity. Probably inactivated by ABI1.

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19955427}.
