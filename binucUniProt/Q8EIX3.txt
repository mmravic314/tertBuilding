CC   -!- FUNCTION: Toxic component of a toxin-antitoxin (TA) module;
CC       overexpression in wild-type temporarily inhibits cell growth,
CC       overexpression in a hipAB deletion leads to acute growth
CC       inhibition. The toxic effect of HipA is neutralized by its cognate
CC       antitoxin HipB. In the ternary phosphoserine-HipA-HipB-DNA complex
CC       the DNA is bent about 125 degrees; all HipA in the crystallized
CC       ternary complex is phosphorylated. In E.coli phosphorylation of
CC       HipA is thought to release HipB from the HipA-HipB-DNA complex,
CC       suggesting the complex functions differently in the 2 bacteria
CC       (PubMed:25056321). Phosphorylates Glu-tRNA-ligase (GltX, on 'Ser-
CC       239') in vivo, with HipB probably acts as a corepressor for
CC       transcription of the hipBA promoter (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P23874, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25056321}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a protein = ADP + a phosphoprotein.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25056321}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. Forms a HipA(2)HipB(2)-DNA complex with cognate
CC       antitoxin HipB; has higher affinity for the latter when HipB is
CC       prebound to DNA and HipA is phosphorylated. Binds DNA in the
CC       ternary complex. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25056321}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expressed in late-exponential phase; part of the hipBA
CC       operon. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25056321}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: 50% decrease in biofilm.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20034561}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the HipA Ser/Thr kinase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
