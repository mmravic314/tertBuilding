CC   -!- FUNCTION: DNA-dependent RNA polymerase catalyzes the transcription
CC       of DNA into RNA using the four ribonucleoside triphosphates as
CC       substrates. Component of RNA polymerase II which synthesizes mRNA
CC       precursors and many functional non-coding RNAs. Pol II is the
CC       central component of the basal RNA polymerase II transcription
CC       machinery. It is composed of mobile elements that move relative to
CC       each other. RPB9 is part of the upper jaw surrounding the central
CC       large cleft and thought to grab the incoming DNA template.
CC       Involved in the regulation of transcription elongation. Involved
CC       in DNA repair of damage in the transcribed strand. Mediates a
CC       transcription-coupled repair (TCR) subpathway of nucleotide
CC       excision repair (NER). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12411509}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the RNA polymerase II (Pol II) complex
CC       consisting of 12 subunits. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11805306,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12914699, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16341226}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus, nucleolus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P32529}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Present with 2440 molecules/cell in log phase SD
CC       medium. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14562106}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the archaeal RpoM/eukaryotic
CC       RPA12/RPB9/RPC11 RNA polymerase family. {ECO:0000305}.
