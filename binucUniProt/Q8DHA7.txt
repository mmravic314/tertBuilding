CC   -!- FUNCTION: One of the components of the core complex of photosystem
CC       II (PSII). PSII is a light-driven water:plastoquinone
CC       oxidoreductase that uses light energy to abstract electrons from
CC       H(2)O, generating O(2) and a proton gradient subsequently used for
CC       ATP formation. It consists of a core antenna complex that captures
CC       photons, and an electron transfer chain that converts photonic
CC       excitation into a charge separation. This subunit is found at the
CC       monomer-monomer interface. Probably involved in dimerization of
CC       PSII; at the monomer-monomer interface the only protein-protein
CC       contacts observed are between the 2 PsbM subunits. Lipids,
CC       chlorophylls and carotenoids contribute strongly to PSII
CC       dimerization. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00438,
