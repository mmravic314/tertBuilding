CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cell wall protein that participates directly in adhesive
CC       cell-cell interactions during yeast flocculation, a reversible,
CC       asexual and Ca(2+)-dependent process in which cells adhere to form
CC       aggregates (flocs) consisting of thousands of cells. The lectin-
CC       like protein sticks out of the cell wall of flocculent cells and
CC       selectively binds mannose residues in the cell walls of adjacent
CC       cells. Activity is inhibited by mannose, but not by glucose,
CC       maltose, sucrose or galactose.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted, cell wall
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9483793}. Membrane {ECO:0000305}; Lipid-
CC       anchor, GPI-anchor {ECO:0000305}. Note=Covalently-linked GPI-
CC       modified cell wall protein (GPI-CWP). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The number of the intragenic tandem repeats varies between
CC       different S.cerevisiae strains. There is a linear correlation
CC       between protein size and the extend of adhesion: the more repeats,
CC       the stronger the adhesion properties and the greater the fraction
CC       of flocculating cells (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- PTM: Extensively O-glycosylated. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- PTM: The GPI-anchor is attached to the protein in the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum and serves to target the protein to the cell surface.
CC       There, the glucosamine-inositol phospholipid moiety is cleaved off
CC       and the GPI-modified mannoprotein is covalently attached via its
CC       lipidless GPI glycan remnant to the 1,6-beta-glucan of the outer
CC       cell wall layer (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: For many industrial applications in which the yeast
CC       Saccharomyces cerevisiae is used, e.g. beer, wine and alcohol
CC       production, appropriate flocculation behavior is one of the most
CC       important characteristics of a good production strain. The ability
CC       of yeast cells to flocculate is of considerable importance, as it
CC       provides an effective, environment-friendly, simple and cost-free
CC       way to separate yeast cells from the fermentation product at the
CC       end of fermentation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12698276,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16487349}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the flocculin family. {ECO:0000305}.
