CC   -!- FUNCTION: Zinc phosphodiesterase, which has both exoribonuclease
CC       and endoribonuclease activities, depending on the nature of the
CC       substrate and of the added divalent cation, and on its 3'-terminal
CC       structure. Can process the 3' termini of both CCA-less and CCA-
CC       containing tRNA precursors. CCA-less tRNAs are cleaved
CC       endonucleolytically after the discriminator base, whereas residues
CC       following the CCA sequence can be removed exonucleolytically or
CC       endonucleolytically in CCA-containing molecules. Does not remove
CC       the CCA sequence. May also be involved in the degradation of
CC       mRNAs. In vitro, hydrolyzes bis(p-nitrophenyl)phosphate and
CC       thymidine-5'-p-nitrophenyl phosphate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12029081, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15764599,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16629673, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19366704,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20489203}.

CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Activated by cobalt and manganese. Strongly

CC       group. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15764599, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19366704,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20489203}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15764599,

CC       similarity with the AtsA family. PubMed:12029081 however showed
CC       that it is a zinc phosphodiesterase. {ECO:0000305}.
