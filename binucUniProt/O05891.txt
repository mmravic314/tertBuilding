CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the reversible phosphorylation of
CC       deoxythymidine monophosphate (dTMP) to deoxythymidine diphosphate
CC       (dTDP), using ATP as its preferred phosphoryl donor. Situated at
CC       the junction of both de novo and salvage pathways of
CC       deoxythymidine triphosphate (dTTP) synthesis, is essential for DNA
CC       synthesis and cellular growth. Has a broad specificity for
CC       nucleoside triphosphates, being highly active with ATP or dATP as
CC       phosphate donors, and less active with ITP, GTP, CTP and UTP.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + dTMP = ADP + dTDP.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420;
CC       Note=Binds 1 Mg(2+) ion per subunit. This ion is required for
CC       catalysis, binding to the active site transiently (at the TMP-
CC       binding site), and probably acting as a clamp between the
CC       phosphoryl donor and acceptor.;
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Competitively inhibited at the phosphate
CC       acceptor site by 3'-azido-3'-deoxythymidine monophosphate (AZT-MP)
CC       (in contrast to other TMPKs such as E.coli, in which it is a good
CC       substrate). Inhibition seems to result from the impossibility of
CC       magnesium binding.
