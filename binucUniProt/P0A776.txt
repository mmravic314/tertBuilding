CC   -!- FUNCTION: Master regulator of 5'-dependent mRNA decay. Accelerates
CC       the degradation of transcripts by removing pyrophosphate from the
CC       5'-end of triphosphorylated RNA, leading to a more labile
CC       monophosphorylated state that can stimulate subsequent
CC       ribonuclease cleavage. Preferentially hydrolyzes diadenosine
CC       penta-phosphate with ATP as one of the reaction products. Also
CC       able to hydrolyze diadenosine hexa- and tetra-phosphate. Has no
CC       activity on diadenosine tri-phosphate, ADP-ribose, NADH and UDP-
CC       glucose. In the meningitis causing strain E.coli K1, has been
CC       shown to play a role in HBMEC (human brain microvascular
CC       endothelial cells) invasion in vitro.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10760174, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18202662}.
