CC   -!- DOMAIN: The channel is composed of 4 repeated units, each
CC       containing a transmembrane pore part and a gating ring part. The
CC       gating ring is composed of eight identical RCK (Regulators of K
CC       conductance) domains, in an alternating arrangement of one domain
CC       from each of the four subunits and four from the intracellular
CC       solution. Two protein interfaces between dimers of RCK domains
CC       from the pore-forming subunit and from the intracellular solution
CC       hold the ring together. One is called the fixed interface and the
CC       other the flexible interface. The flexible interface forms a cleft
CC       where calcium binds. Upon calcium binding the gating ring
CC       undergoes a conformational change that enables it to pull open the
CC       inner helices of the pore, allowing ion conduction.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: It is not known whether calcium is the
