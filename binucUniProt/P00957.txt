CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the attachment of alanine to tRNA(Ala) in a
CC       two-step reaction: alanine is first activated by ATP to form Ala-
CC       AMP and then transferred to the acceptor end of tRNA(Ala). AlaRS
CC       also incorrectly activates the sterically smaller than alanine
CC       amino acid glycine as well as the sterically larger amino acid
CC       serine. These incorrectly charged amino acids occur because the of
CC       inherent physicochemical limitations on discrimination between
CC       closely related amino acids (glycine and serine) in the charging
CC       step.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Edits incorrectly charged Ser-tRNA(Ala) and Gly-
CC       tRNA(Ala) but not incorrectly charged Ser-tRNA(Thr).
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + L-alanine + tRNA(Ala) = AMP +
CC       diphosphate + L-alanyl-tRNA(Ala).
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Zn(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:29105;
CC       Note=Binds 1 zinc ion per subunit; it is not clear where this
CC       binding occurs.;
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7005211}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Self; NbExp=2; IntAct=EBI-544061, EBI-544061;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Consists of three domains; the N-terminal catalytic
CC       domain, the editing domain and the C-terminal C-Ala domain. The
CC       editing domain removes incorrectly charged amino acids, while the
CC       C-Ala domain, along with tRNA(Ala), serves as a bridge to
CC       cooperatively bring together the editing and aminoacylation
CC       centers thus stimulating deacylation of misacylated tRNAs.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The editing domain removes incorrectly charged amino
CC       acids, i.e. Ser-tRNA(Ala) or Gly-tRNA(Ala) become uncharged
CC       tRNA(Ala) and the amino acid. It is specific for the acceptor stem
CC       of tRNA(Ala).
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal C-Ala domain, along with tRNA(Ala), serves
CC       as a bridge to cooperatively bring together the editing and
CC       aminoacylation centers thus stimulating deacylation of misacylated
CC       tRNAs. This C-Ala domain can be replaced in vitro by the
CC       corresponding domain of Aquifex aeolicus or man. Recognition of
CC       tRNA(Ala) by the 2 domains is independent, that is one enzyme
CC       recognizes the same tRNA(Ala) in 2 different manners.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the class-II aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
