CC   -!- FUNCTION: Nucleotidyltransferase that catalyzes the formation of
CC       cyclic GMP-AMP (cGAMP) from ATP and GTP. Catalysis involves both
CC       the formation of a 2',5' phosphodiester linkage at the GpA step
CC       and the formation of a 3',5' phosphodiester linkage at the ApG
CC       step, producing c[G(2',5')pA(3',5')p]. Has antiviral activity by
CC       acting as a key cytosolic DNA sensor, the presence of double-
CC       stranded DNA (dsDNA) in the cytoplasm being a danger signal that
CC       triggers the immune responses. Binds cytosolic DNA directly,
CC       leading to activation and synthesis of cGAMP, a second messenger
CC       that binds to and activates TMEM173/STING, thereby triggering
CC       type-I interferon production. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23258413,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23647843, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23722158}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + GTP = 2 diphosphate + cyclic G-P(2'-
CC       5')A-P(3'-5'). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23647843}.
