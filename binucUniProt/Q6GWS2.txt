CC   -!- FUNCTION: RNA-dependent RNA polymerase which is responsible for
CC       replication and transcription of the viral RNA genome. During
CC       transcription, synthesizes 4 subgenomic RNAs, and assures their
CC       capping by a cap-snatching mechanism, in which cellular capped
CC       pre-mRNA are used to generate primers for viral transcription. The
CC       3'-end of subgenomic mRNAs molecules are heterogeneous and not
CC       polyadenylated. The replicase function is to direct synthesis of
CC       antigenomic and genomic RNA which are encapsidated and non capped.
CC       As a consequence of the use of the same enzyme for both
CC       transcription and replication, these mechanisms need to be well
CC       coordinated. These processes may be regulated by proteins N and Z
CC       in a dose-dependent manner. {ECO:0000256|PIRNR:PIRNR000836}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Nucleoside triphosphate + RNA(n) = diphosphate
CC       + RNA(n+1). {ECO:0000256|PIRNR:PIRNR000836,
CC       ECO:0000256|SAAS:SAAS00276591}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homomultimerizes; the oligomeric structure is essential
CC       for the polymerase activity. Interacts with the nucleocapsid
CC       protein N. Interacts with protein Z; this interaction inhibits
CC       viral transcription and replication.
CC       {ECO:0000256|PIRNR:PIRNR000836}.
