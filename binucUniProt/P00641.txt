CC   -!- FUNCTION: Junction-resolving enzyme that selectively binds and
CC       cleaves four-way (Holliday) DNA junctions present after viral
CC       genomic replication. These intermediates are created during DNA
CC       repair, processing of stalled replication forks and homologous
CC       genetic recombination. Introduces two nicks on the two non-
CC       crossing strands, at 5' sides of the junction. Participates also
CC       together with gp6 in the degradation of host chromosome to provide
CC       nucleotides for phage DNA synthesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12628932,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23207296, ECO:0000269|PubMed:3972821,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9236119}.

CC       phosphooligonucleotide end-products.
