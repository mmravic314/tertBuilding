CC   -!- FUNCTION: Highly processive DNA-dependent RNA polymerase that
CC       catalyzes the transcription of class II and class III viral genes.
CC       Recognizes a specific promoter sequence and enters first into an
CC       "abortive phase" where very short transcripts are synthesized and
CC       released before proceeding to the processive transcription of long
CC       RNA chains. Unwinds the double-stranded DNA to expose the coding
CC       strand for templating. Participates in the initiation of viral DNA
CC       replication presumably by making primers accessible to the DNA
CC       polymerase, thus facilitating the DNA opening. Plays also a role
CC       in viral DNA packaging, probably by pausing the transcription at
CC       the right end of concatemer junction to allow packaging complex
CC       recruitment and beginning of the packaging process.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15223315, ECO:0000269|PubMed:3415967,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:6945573, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7373707,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9192997}.
