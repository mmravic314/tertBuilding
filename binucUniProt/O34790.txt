CC   -!- FUNCTION: Prenyltransferase that catalyzes in vivo the transfer of
CC       the heptaprenyl moiety of heptaprenyl pyrophosphate (HepPP; 35
CC       carbon atoms) to the C3 hydroxyl of sn-glycerol-1-phosphate (G1P),
CC       producing heptaprenylglyceryl phosphate (HepGP). This reaction is
CC       an ether-bond-formation step in the biosynthesis of archaea-type
CC       G1P-based membrane lipids found in Bacillales. To a much lesser
CC       extent, is also able to use geranyl diphosphate (GPP; C10) and
CC       geranylgeranyl diphosphate (GGPP; C20) as the prenyl donors, but
CC       not farnesyl pyrophosphate (FPP; C15). Can not use glycerol-3-
CC       phosphate (G3P) or 3-phosphoglycerate (3PG) as an acceptor.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21214173, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21761520}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: All-trans-heptaprenyl diphosphate + sn-
CC       glycerol 1-phosphate = diphosphate + sn-3-O-(all-trans-
CC       heptaprenyl)glycerol 1-phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21761520}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420; Evidence={ECO:0000250};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Membrane lipid metabolism; glycerophospholipid
CC       metabolism. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21761520}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21761520}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene show a cloggy growth
CC       and do not produce the dephosphorylated and acetylated derivatives
CC       of HepGP. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21761520}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The HepGP product, which is the first archaea-type
CC       G1P-based ether lipid being identified within the phylogenetic
CC       domain of the bacteria, was found to be subsequently
CC       dephosphorylated and acetylated in vivo. However, HepG and its
CC       acetylated derivatives represent only a minor fraction of the
CC       total lipid in B.subtilis.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the GGGP/HepGP synthase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
