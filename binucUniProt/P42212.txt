CC   -!- FUNCTION: Energy-transfer acceptor. Its role is to transduce the
CC       blue chemiluminescence of the protein aequorin into green
CC       fluorescent light by energy transfer. Fluoresces in vivo upon
CC       receiving energy from the Ca(2+)-activated photoprotein aequorin.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Absorption:
CC         Abs(max)=395 nm;
CC         Note=Exhibits a smaller absorbance peak at 470 nm. The
CC         fluorescence emission spectrum peaks at 509 nm with a shoulder
CC         at 540 nm.;
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Photocytes.
CC   -!- PTM: Contains a chromophore consisting of modified amino acid
CC       residues. The chromophore is formed by autocatalytic backbone
CC       condensation between Ser-65 and Gly-67, and oxidation of Tyr-66 to
CC       didehydrotyrosine. Maturation of the chromophore requires nothing
CC       other than molecular oxygen.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Green fluorescent protein has been engineered to
CC       produce a vast number of variously colored mutants, fusion
CC       proteins, and biosensors. Fluorescent proteins and its mutated
CC       allelic forms, blue, cyan and yellow have become a useful and
CC       ubiquitous tool for making chimeric proteins, where they function
CC       as a fluorescent protein tag. Typically they tolerate N- and C-
CC       terminal fusion to a broad variety of proteins. They have been
CC       expressed in most known cell types and are used as a noninvasive
CC       fluorescent marker in living cells and organisms. They enable a
CC       wide range of applications where they have functioned as a cell
CC       lineage tracer, reporter of gene expression, or as a measure of
CC       protein-protein interactions. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17685514}.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Can also be used as a molecular thermometer,
CC       allowing accurate temperature measurements in fluids. The
CC       measurement process relies on the detection of the blinking of GFP
CC       using fluorescence correlation spectroscopy.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17685514}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the GFP family. {ECO:0000305}.
