CC   -!- FUNCTION: Error-prone polymerase lacking a proofreading 3'-5'
CC       exonuclease which plays a role in viral DNA repair. Specifically
CC       binds intermediates in the single-nucleotide base-excision repair
CC       process. Also catalyzes DNA polymerization with low nucleotide-
CC       insertion fidelity. Together with the viral DNA ligase, fills the
CC       single nucleotide gaps generated by the AP endonuclease.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11685239, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12595253}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Deoxynucleoside triphosphate + DNA(n) =
CC       diphosphate + DNA(n+1).

CC       Note=In the presence of magnesium, pol X shows a strong preference
CC       for the ssDNA gaps having one and two nucleotides.;
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Small DNA polymerase formed from only a palm and a C-
CC       terminal subdomain. The total DNA-binding site of pol X is
CC       composed of two DNA-binding subsites.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Consistent with its intracellular location, ASFV
CC       encodes its own replicative DNA polymerase and three base excision
CC       repair enzymes: a class II AP endonuclease, the repair polymerase
CC       Pol X, and an ATP-dependent DNA ligase.
