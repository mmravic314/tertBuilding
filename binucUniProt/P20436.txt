CC   -!- FUNCTION: DNA-dependent RNA polymerases catalyze the
CC       transcription. of DNA into RNA using the four ribonucleoside
CC       triphosphates as substrates. Common component of RNA polymerases
CC       I, II and III which synthesize ribosomal RNA precursors, mRNA
CC       precursors and many functional non-coding RNAs, and small RNAs,
CC       such as 5S rRNA and tRNAs, respectively.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18160037, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24153182,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24153184}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the RNA polymerase I (Pol I), RNA polymerase
CC       II (Pol II) and RNA polymerase III (Pol III) complexes. Component
CC       of the RNA polymerase I (Pol I) complex consisting of 14 subunits:
CC       RPA135, RPA190, RPC40, RPA14, RPB5, RPO26, RPA43, RPB8, RPA12,
CC       RPB10, RPC19, RPC10, RPA49 and RPA34. The complex is composed of a
CC       horseshoe-shaped core containing ten subunits (RPA135, RPA190,
CC       RPB5, RPO26, RPB8, RPB10, RPC10, RPA12, RPC19 and RPC40) where
CC       RPA135 and RPA190 form the DNA-binding cleft. Outside of the core,
CC       RPA14 and RPA43 form the stalk that mediates interactions with
CC       transcription initiation factors and newly synthesized RNA.
CC       Component of the RNA polymerase II (Pol II) complex consisting of
CC       12 subunits: RPO21, RPB2, RPB3, RPB4, RPB5, RPO26, RPB7, RPB8,
CC       RPB9, RPB10 and RPC10. Component of the RNA polymerase III (Pol
CC       III) complex consisting of 17 subunits. Directly interacts with
CC       POLR2A. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11805306, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12407181,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12914699, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16341226,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18160037, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24153182,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24153184}.
