CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic subunit of the poly(A)-nuclease (PAN)
CC       deadenylation complex, one of two cytoplasmic mRNA deadenylases
CC       involved in mRNA turnover. PAN specifically shortens poly(A) tails
CC       of RNA when the poly(A) stretch is bound by poly(A)-binding
CC       protein PAB1, which is followed by rapid degradation of the
CC       shortened mRNA tails by the CCR4-NOT complex. Deadenylated mRNAs
CC       are then degraded by two alternative mechanisms, namely exosome-
CC       mediated 3'-5' exonucleolytic degradation, or deadenlyation-
CC       dependent mRNA decaping by DCP1-DCP2 and subsequent 5'-3'
CC       exonucleolytic degradation by XRN1. May also be involved in post-
CC       transcriptional maturation of mRNA poly(A) tails, trimming the
CC       tails from their synthesized length to the slightly shorter,
CC       apparently messenger-specific length found on newly exported
CC       mRNAs. PAN cooperates with protein kinase DUN1 in the regulation
CC       of RAD5 mRNA levels and cell survival in response to replicational
CC       stress. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11239395, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11953437,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1358757, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15630021,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15894541, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8550599,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8816488, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9774670}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Exonucleolytic cleavage of poly(A) to 5'-AMP.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03182, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1358757}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Positively regulated by the regulatory subunit
CC       PAN3. Negatively regulated by PAB1-binding protein PBP1. Inhibited
CC       under stress conditions. Inhibition of deadenylation under stress
CC       increases mRNA stability, which may be a mechanism to retain the
CC       majority of the cytoplasmic pool of mRNAs for later reuse and
CC       recovery from stress. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03182,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15121841, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16940550,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8816488}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with the regulatory subunit PAN3 to form the
CC       poly(A)-nuclease (PAN) deadenylation complex. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_03182, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15169912,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8816488}.
