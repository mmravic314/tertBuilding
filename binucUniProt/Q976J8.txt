CC   -!- FUNCTION: Carrier protein that bears the covalently bound
CC       substrates for arginine and lysine biosynthesis; bound L-glutamate
CC       is sequentially converted to L-ornithine, while bound alpha-
CC       aminoadipate (AAA) is sequentially converted to L-lysine.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23434852}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Amino-acid biosynthesis; L-lysine biosynthesis via AAA
CC       pathway.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Amino-acid biosynthesis; L-arginine biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23434852}.
CC   -!- PTM: Formation of an isopeptide bond between the gamma-carboxyl
CC       group of the C-terminal glutamate and the amino group of alpha-
CC       aminoadipate (AAA) is catalyzed by LysX. The bound AAA is then
CC       converted to L-lysine in a series of reactions catalyzed by LysZ,
CC       LysY and LysJ. Release of the product L-lysine is catalyzed by
CC       LysK. Formation of an isopeptide bond between the gamma-carboxyl
CC       group of the C-terminal glutamate and the amino group of L-
CC       glutamate is catalyzed by ArgX. The bound substrate is then
CC       sequentially converted to ornithine which is eventually converted
CC       to L-arginine (PubMed:23434852). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23434852}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Binds zinc ions via its zinc finger domain, as
CC       shown by X-ray crystallography (PubMed:23434852). In contrast, the
CC       absorption spectrum of the ortholog from Thermophilus suggests
CC       that it may bind iron ions, possibly via the predicted zinc finger
CC       domain. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:23434852}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 1 TFIIB-type zinc finger. {ECO:0000305}.
