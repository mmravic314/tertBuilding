CC   -!- FUNCTION: Core component of the KaiABC clock protein complex,
CC       which constitutes the main circadian regulator in cyanobacteria.
CC       Binds to DNA. The KaiABC complex may act as a promoter-nonspecific
CC       transcription regulator that represses transcription, possibly by
CC       acting on the state of chromosome compaction.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14709675, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9727980}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a protein = ADP + a phosphoprotein.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:15304218};
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: The interaction with KaiA enhances its
CC       phosphorylation status, while the interaction with KaiB decreases
CC       it. A KaiA dimer is sufficient to enhance KaiC phosphorylation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12391300, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12727878,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12727879}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homohexamer; hexamerization is dependent on ATP-binding.
CC       Core component of the KaiABC complex, at least composed of a KaiC
CC       homohexamer, a KaiB dimer and two KaiA dimers. Interacts directly
CC       with SasA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10064581,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10786837, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11356188,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12727878, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12727879}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Self; NbExp=9; IntAct=EBI-592287, EBI-592287;
CC       Q79PF6:kaiA; NbExp=17; IntAct=EBI-592287, EBI-592281;
CC       Q79PF5:kaiB; NbExp=10; IntAct=EBI-592287, EBI-619150;
CC       Q06904:sasA; NbExp=6; IntAct=EBI-592287, EBI-626872;
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Accumulates in a circadian fashion, peaking
CC       at circadian time (CT) 15-18. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15229218}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Down-regulated by itself.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14709675}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The KaiC domains mediate the interaction with KaiA.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated on serine/threonine residues by autocatalysis.
CC       Both phosphorylated and unphosphorylated forms exist. Can probably
CC       autophosphorylate and autodephosphorylate. Phosphorylated form
CC       correlates with clock speed. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12391300,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12727878, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12727879}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: 'Kai' means 'cycle' in Japanese.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the KaiC family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 2 kaiC domains. {ECO:0000305}.
