CC   -!- FUNCTION: The heterodimer acts as both an ATP-dependent DNA
CC       helicase and an ATP-dependent single-stranded exonuclease, acting
CC       in both directions. Recognizes the B.subtilis chi site (5'-AGCGG-
CC       3') which transforms the enzyme from a helicase which degrades
CC       both DNA strands to one with only 5' to 3' exonuclease activity.
CC       This generates a double-stranded DNA with a protruding 3'-
CC       terminated single-stranded tail suitable for the initiation of
CC       homologous recombination (chi fragment). The AddB nuclease domain
CC       is not required for chi fragment generation; this subunit has 5'
CC       -> 3' nuclease activity. RecA thread formation during DNA double-
CC       strand break repair requires RecJ or AddAB.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10756102, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17570399,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8387145}.
