CC   -!- FUNCTION: Scavenger receptor that displays several functions
CC       associated with host defense. Promotes binding and phagocytosis of
CC       Gram-positive, Gram-negative bacteria and yeast. Binds also to
CC       sialyl Lewis X or a trisaccharide and asialo-orosomucoid (ASOR).
CC       Mediates the recognition, internalization and degradation of
CC       oxidatively modified low density lipoprotein (oxLDL) by vascular
CC       endothelial cells (By similarity). Binds to several carbohydrates
CC       including Gal-type ligands, D-galactose, L- and D-fucose, GalNAc,
CC       T and Tn antigens in a calcium-dependent manner and internalizes
CC       specifically GalNAc in nurse-like cells (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The extracellular domain forms a stable trimer (By
CC       similarity). The extracellular domain interacts with fibrillar
CC       beta amyloid peptide. {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16868960,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17420244}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane {ECO:0000250}; Single-pass type II
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000250}. Note=Forms clusters on the cell
CC       surface. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in vascular endothelial cells in the
CC       heart, in perivascular macrophage and smooth muscle cells.
CC       Expressed in plaques-surrounding reactive astrocytes located in
CC       cerebral cortex and hippocampus and in leptomeningeal vessels
CC       showing characteristics of cerebral amyloid angiopathy (CAA) in a
CC       double transgenic mouse model of Alzheimer disease (at protein
CC       level). Strongly expressed in lung. Moderately expressed in heart,
CC       skeletal muscle, spleen, liver, brain, colon, testis, stomach and
CC       kidney. Expressed in neonatal astrocytes. Expressed in reactive
CC       astrocytes and vascular/perivascular cells in the brain of a
CC       double transgenic mouse model of Alzheimer disease.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11564734, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11718900,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16868960}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed in embryo at 9 dpc, increases
CC       progressively to a peak at 14 dpc and gradually decreases until 19
CC       dpc. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11718900}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated in activated microglia and by fibrillar
CC       beta amyloid peptide in activated astrocytes.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16868960}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 1 C-type lectin domain. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU00040}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 2 collagen-like domains. {ECO:0000305}.
