CC       Gag and Gag-Pol polyproteins during or shortly after the release
CC       of the virion from the plasma membrane. Cleavages take place as an
CC       ordered, step-wise cascade to yield mature proteins. This process
CC       is called maturation. Displays maximal activity during the budding
CC       process just prior to particle release from the cell.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00275}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Reverse transcriptase/ribonuclease H is a
CC       multifunctional enzyme that converts the viral dimeric RNA genome
CC       into dsDNA in the cytoplasm, shortly after virus entry into the
CC       cell. This enzyme displays a DNA polymerase activity that can copy
CC       either DNA or RNA templates, and a ribonuclease H (RNase H)
CC       activity that cleaves the RNA strand of RNA-DNA heteroduplexes in
CC       a partially processive 3' to 5' endonucleasic mode. Conversion of
CC       viral genomic RNA into dsDNA requires many steps. A tRNA binds to
CC       the primer-binding site (PBS) situated at the 5' end of the viral
CC       RNA. RT uses the 3' end of the tRNA primer to perform a short
CC       round of RNA-dependent minus-strand DNA synthesis. The reading
CC       proceeds through the U5 region and ends after the repeated (R)
CC       region which is present at both ends of viral RNA. The portion of
CC       the RNA-DNA heteroduplex is digested by the RNase H, resulting in
CC       a ssDNA product attached to the tRNA primer. This ssDNA/tRNA
CC       hybridizes with the identical R region situated at the 3' end of
CC       viral RNA. This template exchange, known as minus-strand DNA
CC       strong stop transfer, can be either intra- or intermolecular. RT
CC       uses the 3' end of this newly synthesized short ssDNA to perform
CC       the RNA-dependent minus-strand DNA synthesis of the whole
CC       template. RNase H digests the RNA template except for a polypurine
CC       tract (PPT) situated at the 5' end of the genome. It is not clear
CC       if both polymerase and RNase H activities are simultaneous. RNase
CC       H probably can proceed both in a polymerase-dependent (RNA cut
CC       into small fragments by the same RT performing DNA synthesis) and
CC       a polymerase-independent mode (cleavage of remaining RNA fragments
CC       by free RTs). Secondly, RT performs DNA-directed plus-strand DNA
CC       synthesis using the PPT that has not been removed by RNase H as
CC       primers. PPT and tRNA primers are then removed by RNase H. The 3'
CC       and 5' ssDNA PBS regions hybridize to form a circular dsDNA
CC       intermediate. Strand displacement synthesis by RT to the PBS and
CC       PPT ends produces a blunt ended, linear dsDNA copy of the viral
CC       genome that includes long terminal repeats (LTRs) at both ends (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Integrase catalyzes viral DNA integration into the host
CC       chromosome, by performing a series of DNA cutting and joining
CC       reactions. This enzyme activity takes place after virion entry
CC       into a cell and reverse transcription of the RNA genome in dsDNA.
CC       The first step in the integration process is 3' processing. This
CC       step requires a complex comprising the viral genome, matrix
CC       protein and integrase. This complex is called the pre-integration
CC       complex (PIC). The integrase protein removes 2 nucleotides from
CC       each 3' end of the viral DNA, leaving recessed CA OH's at the 3'
CC       ends. In the second step that requires cell division, the PIC
CC       enters cell nucleus. In the third step, termed strand transfer,
CC       the integrase protein joins the previously processed 3' ends to
CC       the 5' ends of strands of target cellular DNA at the site of
CC       integration. The last step is viral DNA integration into host
CC       chromosome (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Gag-Pol polyprotein plays a role in budding and is
CC       processed by the viral protease during virion maturation outside
CC       the cell. During budding, it recruits, in a PPXY-dependent or
CC       independent manner, Nedd4-like ubiquitin ligases that conjugate
CC       ubiquitin molecules to Gag, or to Gag binding host factors.
CC       Interaction with HECT ubiquitin ligases probably link the viral
CC       protein to the host ESCRT pathway and facilitate release (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250}.

CC   -!- DOMAIN: Late-budding domains (L domains) are short sequence motifs
CC       essential for viral particle release. They can occur individually
CC       or in close proximity within structural proteins. They interacts
CC       with sorting cellular proteins of the multivesicular body (MVB)
CC       pathway. Most of these proteins are class E vacuolar protein
CC       sorting factors belonging to ESCRT-I, ESCRT-II or ESCRT-III
CC       complexes. RNA-binding phosphoprotein p12 contains one L domain: a
CC       PPXY motif which potentially interacts with the WW domain 3 of
CC       NEDD4 E3 ubiquitin ligase. PPXY motif is essential for virus
CC       egress. Matrix protein p15 contains one L domain: a PTAP/PSAP
CC       motif, which potentially interacts with the UEV domain of TSG101.
CC       The junction between the matrix protein p15 and RNA-binding
CC       phosphoprotein p12 also contains one L domain: a LYPX(n)L motif
CC       which potentially interacts with PDCD6IP. Both PSAP and LYPX(n)L
CC       domains might play little to no role in budding and possibly drive
CC       residual virus release. contains (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.

CC       cleavage. The polyprotein is cleaved during and after budding,
CC       this process is termed maturation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
