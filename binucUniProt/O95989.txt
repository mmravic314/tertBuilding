CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cleaves a beta-phosphate from the diphosphate groups in
CC       PP-InsP5 (diphosphoinositol pentakisphosphate) and [PP]2-InsP4
CC       (bisdiphosphoinositol tetrakisphosphate), suggesting that it may
CC       play a role in signal transduction. InsP6 (inositol
CC       hexakisphophate) is not a substrate. Acts as a negative regulator
CC       of the ERK1/2 pathway. Also able to catalyze the hydrolysis of
CC       dinucleoside oligophosphates, with Ap6A and Ap5A being the
CC       preferred substrates. The major reaction products are ADP and p4a
CC       from Ap6A and ADP and ATP from Ap5A. Also able to hydrolyze 5-
CC       phosphoribose 1-diphosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9822604}.
