CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a fibronectin-dependent adhesin and invasin.
CC       Binds host (in this case human) fibronectin, plasmin, plasminogen,
CC       and human serum albumin. Where the bacteria adhere to human cells
CC       there is major recruitment of microvilli which seem to fuse to
CC       cover the streptococcal chains. Antibodies to this protein reduce
CC       bacterial growth in human blood.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted, cell wall
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:18974092}; Peptidoglycan-anchor
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:18974092}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene adhere to and invade
CC       human cell lines half as well as wild-type bacteria. They grow
CC       less well in human blood, perhaps because they are more resistant
CC       to phagocytosis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18974092}.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Has potential use as a vaccine component.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18974092}.
