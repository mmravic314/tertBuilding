CC   -!- FUNCTION: Key regulator of striated muscle performance by acting
CC       as the major Ca(2+) ATPase responsible for the reuptake of
CC       cytosolic Ca(2+) into the sarcoplasmic reticulum. Catalyzes the
CC       hydrolysis of ATP coupled with the translocation of calcium from
CC       the cytosol to the sarcoplasmic reticulum lumen. Contributes to
CC       calcium sequestration involved in muscular excitation/contraction.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R429}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + H(2)O + Ca(2+)(Side 1) = ADP + phosphate
CC       + Ca(2+)(Side 2).
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by sarcolipin (SLN), phospholamban
CC       (PLN) and myoregulin (MLN). Reversibly inhibited by phospholamban
CC       (PLN) at low calcium concentrations. Dephosphorylated PLN
CC       decreases the apparent affinity of the ATPase for calcium. This
CC       inhibition is regulated by the phosphorylation of PLN.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R429, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10551848,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8428955}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Associated with sarcolipin (SLN), phospholamban (PLN) and
CC       myoregulin (MLN). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R429,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10551848, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8428955}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endoplasmic reticulum membrane; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein. Sarcoplasmic reticulum membrane; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein.
CC   -!- ALTERNATIVE PRODUCTS:

CC         IsoId=P04191-2; Sequence=VSP_000356;
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Skeletal muscle, fast twitch muscle (type II)
CC       fibers. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:3029125}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Isoform SERCA1A and isoform SERCA1B are
CC       predominantly found in adult and neonatal skeletal muscle
CC       respectively. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:3029125}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Increased contractile activity leads to a decrease in
CC       SERCA1 expression, while decreased contractile activity leads to
CC       an increase in SERCA1 expression.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the cation transport ATPase (P-type)
CC       (TC 3.A.3) family. Type IIA subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
